# Transaction Design: Invoices\n\nThis document details the design for the **Invoice** transaction type within the Revenue Recognition System. It is a core component of the quote-to-cash cycle and directly impacts financial reporting and revenue recognition. It builds upon the foundational concepts outlined in the `transaction-system-design.md` document.\n\n## 1. Purpose and Problem Solved\n\n**Purpose:** An Invoice is a formal, itemized commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer, requesting payment for goods delivered or services rendered. It details the products/services, quantities, agreed prices, payment terms, and total amount due.\n\n**Problems Solved:**\n-   Serves as the official demand for payment from customers.\n-   Tracks accounts receivable and customer balances.\n-   Forms a legal basis for the sales transaction and the customer\'s obligation to pay.\n-   Triggers and manages the payment collection process.\n-   Provides necessary documentation for both seller and buyer accounting.\n-   Can be a key input for recognizing revenue, though it is distinct from the revenue recognition event itself under accrual accounting (ASC 606 / IFRS 15).\n\n## 2. Specific Fields & Data Considerations\n\nInvoices will utilize the common fields from the `Transactions` and `TransactionLines` tables. The `transaction_type` in the `Transactions` table will be set to \'Invoice\'.\n\n### 2.1. Invoice-Specific Header Fields\n\nThese fields would typically reside in a dedicated `InvoiceDetails` extension table (1:1 with `Transactions` where `transaction_type` = \'Invoice\') or as nullable fields in `Transactions`.\n\n-   `transaction_id` (PK, FK to `Transactions.transaction_id`)\n-   `due_date` (DATE, Not Null): Calculated based on `Transactions.transaction_date` and `payment_terms_id`. This field is already in the `Transactions` table but is critically enforced and used for Invoices.\n-   `payment_terms_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to a `PaymentTerms` table): Inherited from Sales Order or specified on the Invoice.\n-   `amount_due` (DECIMAL, Not Null, Calculated): The current outstanding balance on the invoice (Total Invoice Amount - Amount Paid - Credit Memos Applied).\n-   `amount_paid` (DECIMAL, Default: 0, Calculated): Total sum of payments applied to this invoice.\n-   `date_last_payment_applied` (DATE, Nullable): The date the most recent payment was applied.\n-   `last_payment_reference` (VARCHAR(255), Nullable): Reference for the last payment (e.g., check number, Stripe payment ID).\n-   `bill_to_address_id` (UUID, Not Null, FK to an `Addresses` table associated with the `entity_id`).\n-   `ship_to_address_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to an `Addresses` table, if relevant, e.g., for freight charges on invoice).\n-   `is_recurring_invoice` (BOOLEAN, Default: false): Flags if this invoice is part of a recurring billing schedule.\n-   `recurring_invoice_template_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to a `RecurringInvoiceTemplates` table).\n\n### 2.2. Invoice-Specific Line Fields\n\nThese fields extend the common `TransactionLines` model.\n\n-   `transaction_line_id` (PK, FK to `TransactionLines.transaction_line_id` if an extension table)\n-   `billed_from_so_line_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to `TransactionLines.transaction_line_id` of the originating Sales Order line).\n-   `billed_from_fulfillment_line_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to `TransactionLines.transaction_line_id` of the originating Item Fulfillment line, if invoicing from fulfillment).\n-   `previously_billed_quantity` (DECIMAL, Read-only, for context): Quantity of the linked SO line already billed on other invoices (useful for partial billing scenarios).\n-   `service_period_start_date` (DATE, Nullable): For services billed for a specific period (e.g., subscriptions).\n-   `service_period_end_date` (DATE, Nullable): For services billed for a specific period.\n\n## 3. Unique Statuses & Lifecycle\n\nThe `status_id` in the `Transactions` table (for the Invoice) will link to `TransactionStatuses` specific to the \'Invoice\' type.\n\n**Example Statuses for Invoices:**\n-   `Draft`: Invoice created, not yet finalized or sent to the customer.\n-   `Open` / `Awaiting Payment`: Invoice finalized and sent to the customer, payment is pending.\n-   `Partially Paid`: One or more partial payments have been received, but a balance remains.\n-   `Paid in Full`: The full amount of the invoice has been paid.\n-   `Overdue`: The `due_date` has passed, and the invoice is not `Paid in Full`.\n-   `Voided`: The invoice has been cancelled after issuance. This typically requires specific accounting treatment, and often a Credit Memo is preferred for adjustments after posting.\n-   `Closed` / `Written Off`: Invoice is no longer expected to be collected (e.g., bad debt). Requires write-off process.\n\n**Lifecycle / Workflow:**\n1.  **Creation:** Generated from an approved Sales Order, a shipped Item Fulfillment, or directly (for miscellaneous sales or services not tied to an SO).\n2.  **Review & Approval (Optional):** May undergo an internal review before being sent.\n3.  **Issuance:** Sent to the customer (e.g., email with PDF attachment, portal access).\n4.  **Payment Application:** Customer payments are received and applied against the invoice, updating `amount_paid` and `amount_due`. Status changes to \'Partially Paid\' or \'Paid in Full\'. (This could involve a separate `CustomerPayment` transaction type).\n5.  **Collections:** If payment is not received by the `due_date`, the invoice becomes \'Overdue\', and collections processes may be initiated.\n6.  **Adjustments:** If corrections are needed after issuance, a Credit Memo is typically created and applied.\n7.  **GL Impact:** When a (non-deferred) invoice is posted, it typically Debits Accounts Receivable and Credits a Revenue account. If revenue recognition criteria are not yet met (e.g., service not yet rendered), it Credits Deferred Revenue. Payments Debit Cash and Credit Accounts Receivable.\n\n## 4. Key Use Cases\n\n-   Billing a customer for products shipped based on a Sales Order and Item Fulfillment.\n-   Invoicing for a completed phase of a professional service project or a milestone achieved.\n-   Generating recurring monthly/annual invoices for subscription services.\n-   Applying customer payments (received via check, ACH, credit card via Stripe) to open invoices.\n-   Managing accounts receivable aging and dunning processes for overdue invoices.\n\n## 5. User Interface (UI) Considerations\n\n-   **Creation/Edit Form:**\n    *   Ability to \"Generate from Sales Order\" or \"Generate from Item Fulfillment\", which would pre-populate customer info and billable lines/quantities.\n    *   Fields for `due_date`, `payment_terms_id`. If created from SO, these may default.\n    *   Line items showing items, quantities being billed, rates, and amounts. Clear distinction if billing for items not yet fulfilled (advance billing).\n    *   Display of subtotal, taxes, shipping charges (if any), and total invoice amount.\n-   **View Mode:** Clear, professional-looking invoice document (for printing/PDF). Should show `amount_due`, `amount_paid`, payment history summary.\n-   **Actions Available:** \'Save Draft\', \'Approve & Email Invoice\', \'Apply Payment\', \'Generate Credit Memo (from this Invoice)\', \'Void Invoice\' (with strong controls and audit trail), \'View Related Transactions\'.\n-   **List View / Aging Reports:** Accounts Receivable aging report (e.g., current, 30, 60, 90+ days overdue). List of invoices filterable by status, customer, due date, etc.\n\n## 6. Relationships to Other Transaction Types\n\n-   **Sales Order:** An Invoice\'s `created_from_transaction_id` frequently links to a `SalesOrder.transaction_id`. Invoice lines (`billed_from_so_line_id`) link back to specific SO lines.\n-   **Item Fulfillment:** Invoices can also be created from Item Fulfillments, especially if billing occurs post-shipment. Similar linking at header and line levels.\n-   **Customer Payment (Potential New Transaction Type):** Customer Payments are transactions that are applied to one or more Invoices to reduce the `amount_due`. This could be a simple update to the Invoice or a dedicated `CustomerPayment` transaction that then links to Invoices.\n-   **Credit Memo:** A Credit Memo is often created to reduce the amount of a specific Invoice (e.g., for returns, price adjustments after billing). It would link to the original Invoice.\n-   **Journal Entry:** Posting an Invoice (and its subsequent payment or crediting) results in the creation of `RevenueJournalEntries` (or more generic Journal Entries).\n-   **Customer (`Entities` table):** Always associated with a customer entity.\n
## 7. Revenue Recognition Impact\n
-   **Decoupling Billing from Revenue:** It is crucial to understand that an Invoice is primarily a **billing event**, not necessarily a revenue recognition event under accrual accounting standards like ASC 606 / IFRS 15.\n-   **Impact on Deferred Revenue / Contract Assets:**
    *   If an Invoice is issued and payment received *before* the Performance Obligation (POB) is satisfied (e.g., annual subscription billed upfront), the amount is typically credited to a **Deferred Revenue** (Contract Liability) account.
    *   If a POB is satisfied and revenue is recognized *before* an invoice is issued (e.g., monthly service recognized, but billed quarterly), this creates an **Unbilled Receivable** (Contract Asset).
-   **Trigger for POB Assessment (Indirectly):** While the Sales Order is the primary driver for POB identification, the act of invoicing can sometimes align with POB satisfaction for certain types of services or point-in-time deliverables, but this is based on the POB terms, not just the invoice itself.\n-   **Data Input for Revenue Schedules:** The invoiced amounts and items are reconciled against the allocated transaction prices of POBs. The system must ensure that recognized revenue for a POB does not exceed its allocated transaction price, regardless of invoiced amounts (which might include taxes or other non-revenue charges).\n-   The timing of the invoice relative to POB satisfaction determines the initial GL impact (e.g., Dr. A/R, Cr. Revenue; or Dr. A/R, Cr. Deferred Revenue). Subsequent revenue recognition entries will adjust Deferred Revenue.\n
