# Transaction Design: Sales Orders\n\nThis document details the design for the **Sales Order (SO)** transaction type within the Revenue Recognition System. It builds upon the foundational concepts outlined in the `transaction-system-design.md` document.\n\n## 1. Purpose and Problem Solved\n\n**Purpose:** A Sales Order is a binding agreement generated by a seller upon receiving a purchase order or commitment from a customer. It confirms the details of a sale, including specific items, quantities, agreed-upon prices, delivery schedules, and payment terms. It serves as an internal authorization to fulfill and eventually bill for the goods or services.\n\n**Problems Solved:**\n-   Formalizes a customer\'s commitment to purchase.\n-   Authorizes and initiates the fulfillment process (e.g., picking, packing, shipping goods or provisioning services).\n-   Provides a basis for invoicing the customer.\n-   Drives demand planning and inventory management by committing stock or signaling service capacity requirements.\n-   Serves as a critical input for revenue recognition processes, particularly for identifying performance obligations and the transaction price.\n-   Tracks the progress of an order through its lifecycle (e.g., pending fulfillment, partially shipped, fully billed).\n\n## 2. Specific Fields & Data Considerations\n\nSales Orders will utilize the common fields from the `Transactions` and `TransactionLines` tables. The `transaction_type` in the `Transactions` table will be set to \'SalesOrder\'.\n\n### 2.1. SalesOrder-Specific Header Fields\n\nThese fields would typically reside in a dedicated `SalesOrderDetails` extension table (1:1 with `Transactions` where `transaction_type` = \'SalesOrder\') for better normalization, or as nullable fields in `Transactions`.\n\n-   `transaction_id` (PK, FK to `Transactions.transaction_id`)\n-   `customer_po_number` (VARCHAR(100), Nullable, Indexed): The customer\'s Purchase Order number, if applicable.\n-   `shipping_address_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to an `Addresses` table associated with the `entity_id`).\n-   `billing_address_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to an `Addresses` table associated with the `entity_id`).\n-   `requested_delivery_date` (DATE, Nullable): Customer\'s preferred date for receiving goods/services.\n-   `promised_ship_date` (DATE, Nullable): Date the company has committed to ship/commence service.\n-   `actual_ship_date` (DATE, Nullable): Actual date goods were shipped or services commenced (often updated by a linked Item Fulfillment).\n-   `shipping_method_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to a `ShippingMethods` table).\n-   `payment_terms_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to a `PaymentTerms` table, e.g., \'Net 30\', \'Due on Receipt\').\n-   `sales_channel_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to a `SalesChannels` table, e.g., \'Direct Sales\', \'Online Store\', \'Partner Reseller\').\n-   `order_type_id` (UUID, Nullable, FK to an `OrderTypes` table, e.g., \'Standard Order\', \'Subscription Activation\', \'Service Renewal\', \'Trial Order\').\n-   `is_tax_inclusive` (BOOLEAN, Default: false): Indicates if prices on the SO already include tax.\n\n### 2.2. SalesOrder-Specific Line Fields\n\nThese fields extend the common `TransactionLines` model and might be part of the `TransactionLines` table itself if broadly applicable or in a `SalesOrderLineDetails` extension table. Some might be calculated/updated by related processes.\n\n-   `transaction_line_id` (PK, FK to `TransactionLines.transaction_line_id` if an extension table)\n-   `quantity_committed` (DECIMAL, Default: 0): Quantity of the item allocated or reserved from inventory for this SO line.\n-   `quantity_fulfilled` (DECIMAL, Default: 0): Quantity of this line item that has been shipped/delivered (updated by linked Item Fulfillment transactions).\n-   `quantity_invoiced` (DECIMAL, Default: 0): Quantity of this line item that has been included on an Invoice (updated by linked Invoice transactions).\n-   `is_line_closed` (BOOLEAN, Default: false): Indicates if this specific line is considered fully processed (fulfilled and invoiced as much as it will be) and no further action is expected.\n-   `original_commitment_date` (DATE, Nullable): Original promised delivery/service date for this line, if different from header.\n\n## 3. Unique Statuses & Lifecycle\n\nThe `status_id` in the `Transactions` table (for the Sales Order) will link to `TransactionStatuses` specific to the \'SalesOrder\' type.\n\n**Example Statuses for Sales Orders:**\n-   `Pending Approval`: SO entered, awaiting internal checks (e.g., credit limit, fraud review).\n-   `Approved` / `Pending Fulfillment`: SO is approved and ready for the fulfillment process.\n-   `Partially Fulfilled`: Some, but not all, lines or quantities on the SO have been shipped/delivered.\n-   `Fully Fulfilled` / `Pending Billing`: All items on the SO have been shipped/delivered; awaiting invoicing.\n-   `Partially Billed`: Some fulfilled quantities have been invoiced.\n-   `Fully Billed` / `Invoiced`: All billable items/quantities have been invoiced. (Could also mean `Closed` if fulfillment and billing are complete).\n-   `Closed`: The order is complete. All lines are fulfilled and invoiced to the extent they will be, or remaining quantities are cancelled.\n-   `Cancelled`: The entire Sales Order was cancelled before significant fulfillment.\n\n**Lifecycle / Workflow:**\n1.  **Creation:** Can be created manually, or automatically from an \'Accepted\' Estimate or an external system (e.g., e-commerce platform via API).\n2.  **Approval (Optional):** May require internal approval based on value, customer credit status, or other business rules.\n3.  **Commitment/Allocation:** Inventory items are committed or allocated to the SO.\n4.  **Fulfillment:** Triggers the creation of Item Fulfillment transactions as goods are shipped or services are rendered.\ SO status updates to \'Partially Fulfilled\' or \'Fully Fulfilled\'.\n5.  **Invoicing:** Triggers the creation of Invoice transactions for fulfilled items/services. SO status updates to \'Partially Billed\' or \'Fully Billed\'.\n6.  **Closure:** Once fully fulfilled and billed (or remaining parts cancelled), the SO is marked \'Closed\'.\n\n## 4. Key Use Cases\n\n-   Customer confirms an order based on a previously provided estimate.\n-   A new subscription service is sold and needs to be provisioned and billed periodically.\n-   A customer places an order for multiple products, some of which may be on backorder.\n-   Processing orders received from an online store or CRM system.\n-   Managing complex orders with multiple shipments and invoices.\n\n## 5. User Interface (UI) Considerations\n\n-   **Creation/Edit Form:**\n    *   Ability to select a `Customer` and populate `shipping_address_id`, `billing_address_id` (defaulting from customer record).\n    *   Fields for `customer_po_number`, `requested_delivery_date`, `payment_terms_id`, etc.\n    *   Option to \"Create from Estimate\" populating relevant fields and lines.\n    *   Line item grid similar to Estimates, but with visibility into `quantity_committed`, `quantity_fulfilled`, `quantity_invoiced` for each line.\n    *   Clear display of order totals, taxes, and any applicable discounts.\n-   **Actions Available:** \'Save Draft\', \'Submit for Approval\', \'Approve Order\', \'Generate Item Fulfillment\', \'Generate Invoice\', \'Cancel Line\', \'Cancel Order\', \'Close Order\', \'View Related Transactions\'.\n-   **Dashboard/List Views:** Display SOs with their current status, fulfillment progress, billing progress, and links to related fulfillments/invoices.\n\n## 6. Relationships to Other Transaction Types\n\n-   **Estimate:** An SO\'s `created_from_transaction_id` can link to the `Estimate.transaction_id` it was converted from.\n-   **Item Fulfillment:** Item Fulfillments are child records of Sales Orders, detailing the shipment of goods or rendering of services. Each Item Fulfillment header links to one SO header. Item Fulfillment lines link to SO lines.\n-   **Invoice:** Invoices are typically generated from Sales Orders (or Item Fulfillments), billing for the items/services. Invoice headers link to one SO header (or sometimes directly to a Fulfillment). Invoice lines link to SO lines (or Fulfillment lines).\n-   **Customer (`Entities` table):** Always associated with a customer entity.\n-   **Performance Obligation:** Sales Order lines are the primary basis for identifying distinct Performance Obligations (POBs) as per ASC 606 / IFRS 15.\n\n## 7. Revenue Recognition Impact\n\n-   **Contract Identification (ASC 606 Step 1):** A Sales Order is generally considered the contract with the customer, assuming it meets the criteria of a contract (e.g., approved, commitment from both parties, identifiable rights, payment terms established, commercial substance).\n-   **Performance Obligation Identification (ASC 606 Step 2):** The distinct goods or services promised in the `TransactionLines` of the SO are identified as POBs. This system will need logic to group or separate lines into POBs based on item attributes and contract terms.\n-   **Transaction Price Determination (ASC 606 Step 3):** The total amount of the Sales Order (after considering variable consideration like discounts, rebates, returns if material) is the transaction price.\n-   **Transaction Price Allocation (ASC 606 Step 4):** The transaction price is allocated to the identified POBs based on their relative Standalone Selling Prices (SSPs). The `TransactionLines.allocated_transaction_price` field will store this. Each POB will sum the allocated prices of its constituent lines.\n-   **Revenue Recognition Trigger (ASC 606 Step 5):** While the SO initiates the POBs, revenue is recognized as each POB is satisfied. Satisfaction for goods is often tied to Item Fulfillments (transfer of control). For services, it can be over time or upon completion, also often evidenced by service-type fulfillments or milestones.\n
